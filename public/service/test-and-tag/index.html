Legal Requirement

Why Test & Tag Is Mandatory in Queensland

Under Queensland's Work Health and Safety Act 2011 and the Electrical Safety Act 2002, employers and PCBUs have a duty of care to ensure all electrical equipment in the workplace is safe. Failure to comply can result in serious penalties.

Legal Obligation

Queensland's Electrical Safety Act 2002 requires that all electrical equipment in a workplace is electrically safe. Regular testing and tagging to AS/NZS 3760 is the accepted method of demonstrating compliance.

Protect Your People

Faulty electrical equipment is one of the leading causes of workplace fires and electric shock injuries. Testing catches faults before they become dangerous — frayed cords, earth leakage, insulation breakdown.

Avoid Fines & Liability

Non-compliance can result in significant fines from Workplace Health and Safety QLD, and leaves you exposed to liability claims if an incident occurs. Regular testing protects your business and your people.

Insurance Requirements

Many insurance policies require evidence that electrical equipment is regularly tested and maintained. Without current test records, a claim related to electrical equipment could be denied.

HOW IT WORKS

Our 3-Step Test & Tag Process

We come to your site, test everything on the spot, and leave you fully compliant — with documented records.

1

Visual Inspection

Every item is physically inspected for visible damage — frayed cords, cracked plugs, exposed wiring, damaged casings. Items with visible defects are flagged immediately.

2

Electrical Testing

Using calibrated portable appliance testers, we run earth continuity, insulation resistance, and polarity tests on each item to verify it's electrically safe per AS/NZS 3760.

3

Tag & Document

Items that pass receive a durable tag showing the test date, result, tester details, and next retest date. You receive a full written report of all items tested, including any failures.
